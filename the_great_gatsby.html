Book Overview

*The Great Gatsby* by F. Scott Fitzgerald, published in 1925, is a classic American novel set during the Jazz Age in the summer of 1922. The story follows the wealthy and enigmatic Jay Gatsby, who is famous for his extravagant parties in West Egg, New York. Told from the perspective of Nick Carraway, Gatsby's neighbor and a recent Yale graduate, the novel explores themes of wealth, love, social class, and the American Dream.

Jay Gatsby, originally James Gatz from a poor background, amasses a fortune to win back Daisy Buchanan, a woman he fell in love with before she married Tom Buchanan, an arrogant and wealthy man from East Egg. Daisy is enchanted by Gatsby’s lavish lifestyle, but societal expectations and personal insecurities ultimately keep them apart. Their affair culminates in tragedy, revealing the hollow nature of Gatsby's dreams and the moral decay of the society around him.

Through its exploration of ambition, illusion, and moral corruption, *The Great Gatsby* offers a critique of the American Dream and the era’s values, making it a powerful commentary on social issues that still resonate today.
